Market Structure Analysis

GBP/USD has been trading in a broader range between $1.25 and $1.27 throughout February, with a series of advances and declines creating a somewhat choppy price action. After reaching highs around $1.2700 in mid-February, the pair has experienced a gradual decline, forming a series of lower highs and lower lows on the daily chart.

The recent price action shows cable challenging the lower end of its recent trading range, with potential to test the psychologically important $1.25 level if current weakness persists. The declining RSI across multiple timeframes suggests bearish momentum remains in control, though not yet at extreme oversold levels that would typically signal an imminent reversal.

Of particular interest is the declining ADX readings, suggesting that while the short-term trend is bearish, the strength of the trend is relatively weak. This could indicate a potential range-bound scenario rather than the start of a major bearish trend.

Conclusion

GBP/USD is showing signs of short-term weakness within a broader range-bound structure. The technical indicators suggest bearish momentum is in control, though the relatively low ADX readings indicate the trend strength is not particularly strong. This creates a scenario where traders should be prepared for both continuation of the current short-term bearish trend and potential range-bound behavior with bounces from key support levels.

For short-term traders, the current setup favors looking for short entries near resistance levels, while also being alert to potential reversal signals if price approaches the strong support zone around $1.25. Swing and position traders might benefit from a more balanced approach, preparing for both scenarios within the broader $1.24-$1.28 range that has contained most price action in recent months.
