Market Structure Analysis

XRP has been in a downtrend since early March, forming a sequence of lower highs and lower lows following the rejection at the $2.95 level. The price is currently testing a critical support zone around $2.05-$2.07, which if broken could accelerate selling pressure toward the $1.95-$2.00 area. The technical structure shows XRP/USD trading below key moving averages on shorter timeframes, which is a negative sign for near-term momentum.

While the primary trend on the daily chart remains bullish (price still above 200 EMA), the medium-term trend has turned bearish with price below the 50 EMA. Technical indicators show mixed conditions across timeframes, with shorter timeframes approaching oversold conditions while daily timeframe shows neutral readings with bearish bias. The MACD shows negative momentum across all timeframes, confirming the bearish bias currently in control.

Bearish Factors

  • • Price below 50 EMA on daily chart and both 20/50 EMAs on shorter timeframes
  • • Negative MACD readings across multiple timeframes

High-Impact Events Analysis

President Trump Speaks (March 11, Tentative): Any comments on cryptocurrency policy or the SEC's approach to digital assets could significantly impact XRP prices. Given XRP's unique regulatory situation, Trump's statements about the SEC or crypto regulation could trigger substantial volatility.

US CPI m/m (March 12, 20:30 UTC): This inflation reading is crucial for determining Federal Reserve monetary policy. The forecast shows a decrease to 0.3% from the previous 0.5%. A higher-than-expected reading could strengthen the dollar and pressure XRP further, while a lower reading might provide support as it could signal earlier rate cuts.

Unemployment Claims (March 13, 20:30 UTC): The forecast of 226K (vs previous 221K) suggests a slight weakening in the labor market. A significantly higher number would indicate economic softening, potentially accelerating Federal Reserve rate cuts, which would generally be bullish for risk assets including XRP.

UoM Consumer Sentiment (March 14, 22:00 UTC): The forecast of 63.8 (vs previous 64.7) indicates slightly decreasing consumer confidence. A reading significantly below expectations could trigger risk-off sentiment initially, but might ultimately support cryptocurrencies if it accelerates expectations for monetary easing.

Conclusion

XRP is showing bearish pressure, trading below key moving averages and approaching critical support at $2.05. The immediate challenge for bulls is defending the $2.05 support zone, which if broken could accelerate the downtrend toward the $1.95-$2.00 area.
