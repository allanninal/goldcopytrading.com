Market Structure Analysis

Litecoin has been under pressure since early March, declining from highs of around $130 to current levels near $105. The cryptocurrency has found some buying interest near the $100 psychological level but is struggling to establish a convincing recovery. The price structure shows lower highs and lower lows on multiple timeframes, confirming a short to medium-term downtrend.

The technical structure shows LTC/USD trading below all major moving averages on short and medium timeframes, which is a bearish signal. The 50/200 EMA relationship is bearish on all timeframes examined, providing overhead resistance for any attempted recovery.

The technical indicators predominantly show bearish to neutral readings. The RSI is in bearish territory across timeframes, suggesting continued selling pressure, though the 15-minute chart is approaching oversold conditions which could trigger a short-term bounce. The MACD shows negative momentum across shorter timeframes, although some potential divergence on the 4-hour chart hints at possible slowing downside momentum. The ADX readings suggest developing trend strength across timeframes, confirming the bearish bias.

Key factors currently influencing Litecoin prices include the recent volatility following Trump's US crypto reserve announcement, the cryptocurrency's network metrics showing steady transaction volume, and its correlation with Bitcoin, which has also experienced recent weakness.
