Market Structure Analysis

Ethereum has been in a sustained downtrend since late December 2024, with the price forming a series of lower highs and lower lows on the daily timeframe. The breach below the 200-day EMA in February marked a significant bearish development, confirming the medium-term downtrend.

The technical structure shows ETH/USD trading below all major moving averages on the daily chart, which is a negative sign for medium-term momentum. The 50/200 EMA relationship remains bullish (no death cross yet), but the declining slope of the 50 EMA suggests this could change if the current price action continues.

The technical indicators predominantly show bearish readings on the daily timeframe, with MACD firmly negative and RSI below the neutral 50 level. However, the 38.43 RSI reading suggests the possibility of a technical bounce if support at $2,100 holds. The short-term indicators (1H and 15M) show more neutral readings, suggesting consolidation may continue before the next major move.

Conclusion

Ethereum is currently showing a bearish bias on the daily timeframe, trading below both the 50-day and 200-day EMAs. The immediate technical structure suggests consolidation between $2,100-$2,200, with short-term momentum relatively neutral but medium-term momentum remaining negative.

The upcoming week features significant market-moving events, particularly Fed Chair Powell's speech today and CPI data on March 12. These events will likely determine the next directional move for ETH/USD. The technical picture would improve with a break above $2,200 and reclaiming the 20-day EMA, while a break below $2,100 would likely accelerate the downside move.

Given the mixed technical signals across different timeframes and the upcoming high-impact events, a cautious approach is warranted. For the immediate term (next 24-48 hours), traders should focus on the reaction to Powell's speech and prepare for potential volatility. For the medium term (next week), the CPI data will likely set the tone for ETH's price action, potentially determining whether the recent stabilization turns into a recovery or further decline.
