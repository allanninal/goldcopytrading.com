Trading Strategy

Buy Strategy

Entry Conditions:

Oversold Bounce Setup:
  • RSI dips below 30 on 4H chart
  • Price forms a bullish engulfing or hammer candle
  • MACD begins to show bullish divergence
  • Entry at confirmation of the reversal candle

Risk Management:

  • Stop Loss: Place below the nearest swing low (~$2,100)
  • Position Sizing: Maximum 2% risk per trade
  • Take Profit Targets:
    • TP1: $2,400 (Previous support level) - 40% of position
    • TP2: $2,600 (50 EMA on 4H) - 40% of position
    • TP3: $2,800 (Major resistance level) - 20% of position

Sell/Short Strategy

Entry Conditions:

Continuation Short Setup:
  • Price rejects at key resistance levels ($2,300-$2,400)
  • RSI shows overbought conditions (>70) on lower timeframes
  • Bearish engulfing pattern at resistance
  • Entry after confirmation of rejection

Risk Management:

  • Stop Loss: Place above the nearest swing high (~$2,400)
  • Position Sizing: Maximum 2% risk per trade
  • Take Profit Targets:
    • TP1: $2,100 (Recent support) - 30% of position
    • TP2: $2,000 (Psychological level) - 50% of position
    • TP3: $1,900 (Extended target) - 20% of position

Additional Considerations

Bullish Scenarios

  • A daily close above $2,400 would suggest a potential trend reversal
  • RSI divergence on the 4H chart could precede a significant bounce
  • Increased buying volume would confirm bullish momentum

Bearish Scenarios

  • Continued closes below $2,200 increase probability of testing $2,000
  • Rising global market uncertainty could accelerate the downtrend
  • Lack of buying volume suggests limited upside potential in near term

Market Context

Ethereum's price action should be considered within the broader crypto market context:

Conclusion

Ethereum is currently in a clear downtrend across multiple timeframes. The most probable scenario is continued bearish pressure with potential tests of lower support levels. However, the market is showing early signs of stabilization, which could lead to a relief bounce. Traders should maintain caution and adhere to strict risk management principles in this volatile environment.
