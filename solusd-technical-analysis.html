Market Structure Analysis

Solana has been in a clear downtrend since early March, forming a sequence of lower highs and lower lows following the rejection at the $180 level. The price is currently testing a critical support zone around $115-$116, which if broken could accelerate selling pressure toward the $108-$110 area. The technical structure shows SOL/USD trading below all major moving averages across multiple timeframes, which is a negative sign for near-term momentum.

The technical indicators predominantly show deeply oversold conditions across all timeframes, with RSI under 30 on multiple timeframes. This suggests a potential short-term relief rally is possible, though the primary trend remains bearish. The MACD shows negative momentum across all timeframes, confirming the strong bearish bias currently in control.

Notably, the price is testing the $115-$116 support zone after a significant decline from the previous analysis level of $138. The volume patterns show increasing selling pressure during downward movements and weak buying during recovery attempts, indicating bears remain firmly in control. The declining ADX reading on the daily timeframe (28.71) suggests some potential weakening of the downtrend, though it remains strong enough to maintain bearish momentum.
