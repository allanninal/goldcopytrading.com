Market Structure Analysis

Solana has been declining since the price rejection at the $155 level, forming a series of lower highs and lower lows. The broader market structure shows SOL/USD trading below all major moving averages, which is a bearish sign for near-term momentum. The 50/200 EMA relationship remains bearish on daily timeframes, with price trading significantly below these levels.

The technical indicators predominantly show bearish to neutral readings. The RSI is in bearish territory on multiple timeframes, though approaching oversold levels on short timeframes, suggesting a potential short-term bounce. The MACD shows negative momentum across timeframes, confirming the bearish bias. The ADX readings suggest a moderately strong trend on the daily chart but weaker trend conditions on lower timeframes, typical of consolidation phases before potential continuation.

Notably, the price is forming a potential consolidation in the $137-$140 range, which could serve as a base for a short-term bounce or a continuation pattern for further downside. The falling volume during this consolidation suggests a lack of buyer conviction, which typically precedes further downside unless significant buying volume emerges.
