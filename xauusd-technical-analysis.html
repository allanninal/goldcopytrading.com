Market Structure Analysis

Gold has been in a strong uptrend since late 2023, establishing a series of higher highs and higher lows on the weekly timeframe. After reaching all-time highs near $2,800 in early January 2025, the market experienced a brief consolidation before extending gains to the recent peak of $2,930 in mid-February.

The current price action represents a normal technical correction within the broader uptrend, with gold pulling back to test previous breakout areas as support. The $2,840-$2,850 zone represents a critical support area that aligns with the previous resistance that should now function as support according to price action theory.

While the short-term market structure has shifted bearish with lower highs and lower lows on the 1-hour and 4-hour charts, the longer-term bullish structure remains intact as long as price maintains above the $2,800 level. The current stabilization attempts near $2,860 suggest sellers may be losing momentum, but a clear reversal pattern has yet to form.

Trading Strategy

For Short-Term Traders (1-3 days)

Entry Strategy: Look for intraday consolidation patterns that form around the $2,850-$2,860 level before entering long positions. Consider bullish divergences on hourly charts as entry triggers.

Alternative Entry: For short-term counter-trend traders, consider selling rallies to the $2,880-$2,890 resistance zone with tight stops above $2,900.

Stop Loss: For longs, place stops below the nearest intraday swing low or below $2,845. For shorts, place stops above $2,900.

Take Profit Targets: TP1 at $2,880, TP2 at $2,900, and TP3 at $2,920 for longs. For shorts, TP1 at $2,840 and TP2 at $2,825.

Risk Management: Risk no more than 1% of capital on any single short-term trade and use a minimum 1:2 risk-reward ratio.

For Swing Traders (1-2 weeks)

Entry Strategy: Use a scale-in approach, with 50% position on a successful retest of the $2,830-$2,840 support zone and the remaining 50% on confirmation of a reversal with a daily close above $2,880.

Stop Loss: Place stops below $2,820, which is below the key support zone.

Take Profit Targets: Primary target at $2,920-$2,930 (previous high) with extended targets at $2,950 and $3,000 (psychological level).

Risk Management: Risk no more than 2% of capital with a minimum 1:2 risk-reward ratio. Consider trailing stops after price moves beyond the $2,900 level.

For Position Traders (1-3 months)

Strategy: Maintain an overall bullish bias as long as price holds above the $2,800 level on the weekly chart.

Entry Zones: Consider dollar-cost averaging in the $2,840-$2,870 range, with additional positions on confirmation of trend resumption above $2,900.

Stop Loss: Use a wider stop below $2,780, which marks the previous major support level.

Take Profit: Consider partial profit-taking at $3,000 (psychological level) and $3,100 (projection target), while maintaining a portion of the position for potentially higher targets.

Risk Management: Allocate no more than 5% of the portfolio to gold positions, with individual entry points risking no more than 2% of total capital.

Trade Execution Plan

Step 1: Short-Term Approach

Wait for a consolidation pattern to form in the $2,850-$2,860 zone before entering long positions. Look for candlestick patterns such as hammers, bullish engulfing patterns, or double bottoms on the 1-hour timeframe as confirmation signals.

Step 2: Medium-Term Approach

For larger positions, wait for a daily close above the 20 EMA on the 4-hour chart ($2,882) to confirm the resumption of the uptrend. Consider adding to positions on a break and hold above $2,900.

Step 3: Position Sizing

Use smaller position sizes than normal due to increased volatility. For short-term trades, risk 0.5-1% of capital; for swing trades, risk 1-1.5%; for position trades, use dollar-cost averaging with 0.5% of capital at each entry point.

Step 4: Exit Strategy

Use a trailing stop strategy once price moves 1.5x your risk. For trend-following entries above $2,880, trail stops below each 4-hour candle low as price advances. Take partial profits at resistance levels ($2,900, $2,930, $3,000).

Remember: Never chase the market after strong moves—wait for pullbacks to key support levels or confirmation of trend continuation.

Risk Factors to Monitor

Economic data surprises, especially from the US, could trigger sharp moves in gold prices. Watch for upcoming inflation reports, labor market data, and GDP revisions.

Comments from Federal Reserve officials could quickly shift market expectations regarding the interest rate path, impacting gold prices. Pay attention to upcoming speeches and Fed minutes.

Changes in real yields (nominal yields minus inflation expectations) have a strong inverse correlation with gold prices. Monitor the TIPS market for shifts in real yield expectations.

The $2,830-$2,840 support zone is critical for maintaining the bullish bias. A decisive break below this level could trigger a deeper correction toward $2,800 or lower.

Conclusion

Gold is currently in a short-term bearish phase within a longer-term bullish market structure. The recent correction from $2,930 to the $2,840 level represents a healthy technical pullback within the broader uptrend that has dominated since late 2023.

While immediate momentum favors bears across multiple timeframes, the oversold conditions developing on the 4-hour chart suggest we may be approaching a potential stabilization or reversal point. Traders should watch the critical $2,830-$2,840 support zone closely, as a successful defense of this area could provide attractive long entries for all timeframes.

The fundamental backdrop for gold remains supportive with expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts in 2025, persistent inflation concerns, and ongoing geopolitical tensions. However, traders should remain vigilant regarding economic data releases and Fed commentary that could shift interest rate expectations and impact gold prices in the near term.

Overall Technical Outlook: Short-term bearish, medium to long-term bullish above $2,800.
