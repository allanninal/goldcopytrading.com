Market Structure Analysis

Gold has experienced increased volatility in recent weeks, with a significant uptrend followed by a sharp correction and subsequent consolidation. The price has formed a potential double bottom around the $2,840 level before recovering to its current position near $2,890. This recovery suggests buyers remain interested at lower levels, but the upside momentum has been limited by resistance around $2,900-$2,910.

The technical indicators show mixed signals across timeframes. The 15-minute chart MACD displays positive momentum, while the 1-hour chart shows minor weakness. The 4-hour chart reveals potential bullish momentum building with positive MACD divergence. RSI readings are largely neutral, with the 1-hour timeframe showing the most bullish bias at 57.89/61.76.

Most notably, the ADX readings are relatively low across shorter timeframes (14.29 on 15-minute, 13.36 on 1-hour), indicating a lack of strong directional commitment – typical of consolidation phases. The higher ADX on the 4-hour chart (36.02) suggests the longer-term trend remains relevant and may reassert itself.

Conclusion

Gold is currently in a consolidation phase following a period of high volatility. The technical picture is mixed across timeframes but suggests building bullish momentum on the 4-hour chart. The immediate range between $2,880-$2,900 is crucial for determining the next directional move.

The recent double bottom formation near $2,840 provides a solid foundation for potential upside if the broader market environment remains supportive. However, resistance at $2,900-$2,910 needs to be decisively broken for the rally to continue toward $2,950-$3,000.

Given today's high-impact economic events and the current consolidation pattern, a measured approach is warranted. The most prudent strategy would be to wait for a clear breakout from the current range before establishing significant positions, while carefully managing risk exposure.
