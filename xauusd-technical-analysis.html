Gold has been consolidating after reaching historic highs in early 2025 and subsequently pulling back. Conclusion

Gold is currently in a consolidation phase following significant volatility in late February. The precious metal appears to be establishing support above the $2,900 level while facing resistance near $2,925-$2,940.

The key level to watch is $2,925 for bulls, as a decisive break above this resistance would signal potential continuation toward the psychologically important $3,000 level. For bears, the $2,900 support zone is critical, as a breakdown would suggest further weakness toward $2,860 or below.

The contrasting signals on different timeframes suggest traders should approach gold with measured caution. With several important economic events scheduled for today, additional volatility could emerge, making proper risk management essential.

Given the neutral technical signals on shorter timeframes but slightly bullish bias on the 4-hour chart, a balanced approach is warranted. The most prudent strategy would be to wait for clear breakout or breakdown signals before establishing significant positions, while being aware of potential headline risks that could shift sentiment in this market.
