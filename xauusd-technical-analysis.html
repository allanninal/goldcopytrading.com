Market Structure Analysis

Gold has experienced a significant correction from its February high of nearly $2,950, dropping to around $2,840 in late February, representing approximately a 3.7% decline. This pullback comes after a strong uptrend that dominated most of January and early February.

The 4-hour chart reveals a clear downtrend with lower highs and lower lows, but the most recent price action shows potential signs of exhaustion in the selling pressure. The deeply oversold RSI readings on the 4-hour chart combined with price finding support at the lower Bollinger Band suggest a potential reversal or at least a short-term bounce may be developing.

What's particularly notable is the strong ADX reading across multiple timeframes, indicating that the recent bearish trend has been powerful. However, the improving short-term momentum indicators (15-minute RSI and MACD) hint at a potential shift in sentiment.
