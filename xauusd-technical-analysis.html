Market Structure Analysis

Gold has undergone a significant shift in momentum over the past week. After the sharp correction from nearly $2,950 to $2,840, XAU/USD has staged a recovery and appears to be consolidating in the $2,880-$2,900 range. The higher timeframes show a broader uptrend that has been interrupted by this correction, but the structure remains bullish as long as the $2,840 support holds.

The technical indicators present a mixed picture in the immediate term, with RSI readings in neutral territory across most timeframes. The MACD shows positive divergence on the 4-hour chart, suggesting potential for renewed upside momentum. The low ADX readings on the shorter timeframes indicate a consolidation phase that could resolve in either direction depending on upcoming economic catalysts.

Price is trading near the middle Bollinger Band on multiple timeframes, further confirming the current equilibrium state. The 50 EMA on the 1-hour chart at $2,879.972 appears to be providing key support, while the recent recovery has established a higher low compared to the $2,840 bottom, which is a constructive sign for bulls.

Conclusion

Gold is currently in a consolidation phase following a correction from recent highs and subsequent recovery. The technical picture appears neutral to slightly bullish in the short term, with the market seeking direction from upcoming economic events.

The $2,875-$2,900 range represents the immediate battleground, with a break above $2,900 potentially signaling a continuation of the broader uptrend toward $2,950. Conversely, a failure to hold $2,875 could trigger a retest of the $2,840-$2,850 support zone.

Given today's high-impact economic events and the current consolidation pattern, a measured approach is warranted. The most prudent strategy would be to wait for clear breakout signals from the current range before establishing significant positions, while carefully managing risk exposure in this transitional market phase.
