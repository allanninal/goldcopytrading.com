Market Structure Analysis

Gold has experienced increased volatility in recent weeks, with a significant uptrend followed by a sharp correction and subsequent consolidation. The price has formed a potential double bottom around the $2,840 level before recovering to its current position near $2,910. This recovery suggests buyers remain interested at lower levels, but the upside momentum has been limited by resistance around $2,900-$2,910.

The technical indicators show mixed signals across timeframes. The 15-minute chart MACD displays positive momentum, while the 1-hour chart shows minor weakness. The 4-hour chart reveals potential bullish momentum building with positive MACD divergence. RSI readings are largely neutral, with the 1-hour timeframe showing the most bullish bias at 55.37/57.01.

Most notably, the ADX readings are relatively low across shorter timeframes (16.92 on 1-hour), indicating a lack of strong directional commitment – typical of consolidation phases. The higher ADX on the 4-hour chart (27.02) suggests the longer-term trend remains relevant and may reassert itself.

Conclusion

Gold has successfully broken out of its previous consolidation range and now displays a clear bullish bias across multiple timeframes. With technical indicators aligned in a bullish configuration, the path of least resistance appears to be higher in the near to medium term.

For traders, a measured approach of buying pullbacks rather than chasing the current move would provide better risk-reward scenarios. Key levels to monitor are the $2,920-$2,930 resistance zone and the newly established support at $2,890-$2,900.
