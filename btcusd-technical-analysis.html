Bitcoin (BTC) is currently trading at approximately $85,657, showing signs of recovery after a significant downtrend from $100,000 to $76,000 levels in February. The asset has entered a consolidation phase with modest recovery attempts from recent lows.

1-Hour Chart

  • Moving Averages: Price below key moving averages but showing recovery signs

  • Price Action: BTC in recovery phase within larger downtrend
  • Moving Averages: Price below both 50 and 200 EMAs
  • RSI: Readings of 47.47 and 34.58, improving from oversold conditions
  • MACD: In negative territory but showing potential reversal signs
  • Volume: Patterns suggest accumulation at lower levels

Key Support and Resistance Levels

Support Levels

  • $83,800 - 50 EMA on 1H chart
  • $82,000 - Previous consolidation low
  • $76,000 - Major support (recent low)

Resistance Levels

  • $87,000 - Short-term resistance
  • $89,000 - Previous support turned resistance
  • $92,000 - Major resistance level

Trading Strategy

Buy Strategy

Entry Points:

Conservative Entry ($83,800-84,000):
  • Wait for pullback to 50 EMA on 1H chart
  • Confirm with RSI bounce from 40-45 level
  • Look for positive MACD histogram turn
Aggressive Entry ($85,200-85,400):
  • Enter on consolidation range break and retest
  • Confirm with RSI above 55
  • Look for increasing breakout volume
Breakout Entry ($87,000-87,500):
  • Wait for break above 200 EMA on 4H
  • Confirm with RSI above 60
  • Look for MACD crossing above signal

Sell/Short Strategy

Entry Points:

Short-term Resistance ($86,500-87,000):
  • Enter at upper Bollinger Band touch
  • Confirm with RSI approaching 70
  • Look for bearish momentum divergence
Major Resistance ($89,000-90,000):
  • Enter on price rejection at resistance
  • Confirm with decreasing volume
  • Look for bearish candlestick patterns
Trend Continuation ($85,000-85,200):
  • Short on 50/200 EMA cross failure
  • Confirm with bearish MACD crossover
  • Look for increasing sell volume

Risk Management

  • Set stop losses 2-3% above entry for short positions
  • Set stop losses 2-3% below entry for long positions
  • Target profit-taking at previous support/resistance levels
  • Consider scaling in/out of positions rather than all-in entries
  • Monitor volume patterns for trade confirmation

Additional Considerations

  • Bitcoin is in a recovery phase but still within a larger downtrend
  • Key resistance levels at 88,000, 92,000, and 96,000 should be monitored
  • Volume patterns suggest accumulation at lower levels
  • Watch for sudden volatility increases that could signal trend changes

Conclusion

Bitcoin is showing signs of recovery within a larger downtrend. While short-term momentum indicators suggest potential upside, traders should remain cautious and adhere to strict risk management principles. The market structure suggests a possible range-bound environment between $83,800 and $87,000 in the near term.
