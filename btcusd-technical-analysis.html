Market Structure Analysis

Bitcoin has been in a corrective phase since reaching peaks above $100,000 in late 2024. The correction appears to be finding support around the 200-day EMA, a critical long-term technical level that often determines the overall market trend. The breach below the 50-day EMA in February marked a significant short-term bearish development.

The technical structure shows BTC/USD trading below the 50-day EMA but finding support near the 200-day EMA, which is typical behavior during bull market corrections. The weekly 20 EMA at approximately $89,854 is currently acting as resistance, with price unable to sustain moves above this level in recent weeks.

The technical indicators predominantly show bearish readings on the daily timeframe, with MACD firmly negative and RSI below the neutral 50 level. However, the 41.81 RSI reading suggests the possibility of a technical bounce if support at the 200-day EMA holds. The shorter timeframes (1H and 15M) show more neutral to slightly bullish readings, suggesting consolidation may continue before the next major move.

High-Impact Events Analysis

Fed Chair Powell Speech (March 8, 17:30 UTC): Powell's comments on monetary policy and inflation could significantly impact Bitcoin. If Powell signals fewer rate cuts than previously expected due to persistent inflation, this could pressure BTC prices as higher rates generally reduce risk appetite for assets like cryptocurrencies. Conversely, dovish comments suggesting an accelerated rate-cutting schedule would likely support BTC prices.

President Trump Speeches (March 8, 16:00 & 20:00 UTC): Given Trump's previous comments on cryptocurrency, particularly his support for a US crypto reserve, any additional statements on crypto policy could create volatility in BTC markets. Positive comments specifically mentioning Bitcoin could trigger a short-term rally, while negative comments could lead to selling pressure.

CPI Data (March 12): This represents the most significant economic release for the week. The forecast shows slight moderation in inflation (Core CPI at 0.3% vs previous 0.4%, annual CPI at 2.9% vs previous 3.0%). If inflation comes in higher than expected, it could pressure BTC prices by reducing expectations for Fed rate cuts. Lower-than-expected inflation could boost BTC by supporting the narrative for monetary easing, which typically benefits risk assets.

UoM Consumer Sentiment and Inflation Expectations (March 14): These metrics provide insight into consumer psychology and inflation outlook. Deteriorating sentiment combined with elevated inflation expectations could create a challenging environment for risk assets like BTC.

MVRV Analysis

The current Market Value to Realized Value (MVRV) ratio for Bitcoin is in neutral territory, approximately between 2.0-2.5. Conversely, the ratio has not approached the sub-1.0 levels that typically indicate market bottoms, suggesting the current correction is not yet near historical oversold conditions.
